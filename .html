MORI ART MUSEUM

4.3/5 stars rating

The Mori Art Museum is one of Tokyo's most iconic contemporary art spaces.Located at the top of the Roppongi Hills Mori Tower, it's famous not just for its cutting-edge exhibits,but also for its incredible views of the city.People visit the Mori Art Museum because it offers a unique "all-in-one" experience that combines world-class contemporary art with some of the best views in Tokyo. Since it's located on the 53rd floor of a skyscraper, your visit usually includes access to an observation deck where you can see landmarks like Tokyo Tower and Mount Fuji. Unlike traditional museums, it stays open late until 10:00 PM, making it a popular spot for a night out to enjoy interactive, "Instagrammable" exhibits and the glittering city lights.


  • ✨ Location: 53F, Roppongi Hills Mori Tower, Roppongi, Minato City.
  • ✨ Opening Hours: 10:00 AM - 10:00 PM (daily)
  • ✨ Entry Fee: ¥1,200 (adults), ¥800 (children)
  • ✨ Contact: +81 3-5770-1234

21_21 DESIGN SIGHT

4.4/5 stars rating

21_21 Design Sight is a cool museum in Tokyo where people go to see how design and art shape our world. Most visitors come to see the amazing building, which has a giant metal roof that looks like a folded piece of cloth. Inside, the exhibits aren't just about old things; they show how everyday objects can be turned into something special. It is located in a beautiful park, making it a great place to relax and get inspired. People love it because it's a modern, creative space that feels very different from a typical museum. It's the perfect spot for anyone who wants to see the future of Japanese design.


  • ✨ Location: 9-7-6 Akasaka, Minato City.
  • ✨ Opening Hours: 10:00 AM - 7:00 PM (closed on Tuesdays)
  • ✨ Entry Fee: ¥1,000 (adults), ¥500 (students)
  • ✨ Contact: +81 3-3475-2121

NEZU MUSEUM

4.5/5 stars rating

The Nezu Museum in Tokyo is a quiet and peaceful place to visit. Its most attractive feature is the beautiful Japanese garden, where you can walk along shady paths, see bamboo groves, and watch colorful koi fish swim in a pond. Inside, the museum displays old Japanese and East Asian art, including lovely ceramics, calligraphy, and rare treasures. The building itself, designed by a famous architect, feels calm and airy, with lots of natural light. People come here to escape the busy city, relax in nature, and enjoy art in a soothing setting.


  • ✨ Location: 6-5-1 Minami-Aoyama, Minato City.
  • ✨ Opening Hours: 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM (closed on Mondays)
  • ✨ Entry Fee: ¥1,000 (adults), ¥500 (students)
  • ✨ Contact: +81 3-3400-2536

SHITAMACHI MUSEUM

4.0/5 stars rating

The Shitamachi Museum in Tokyo offers visitors a unique journey into the everyday life of old Edo, showcasing the culture of ordinary people through immersive and realistic exhibits. Located in Ueno Park, it features life-size replicas of traditional houses, shops, and narrow streets that recreate the atmosphere of Tokyo from over a century ago. Visitors can explore detailed displays such as a merchant's home, a sweet shop, and a coppersmith's workshop, all filled with authentic objects.The museum also highlights daily life through collections of toys, tools, and household items, some of which visitors can interact with.People visit the museum because it provides a nostalgic and hands-on experience, allowing them to step back in time and better understand Tokyo's cultural roots.Its charm lies in its simplicity and authenticity, making it a favorite spot for those interested in history and traditional Japanese life.


  • ✨ Location: 1-1 Ueno Park, Taito City.
  • ✨ Opening Hours: 9:30 AM - 5:00 PM (closed on Mondays)
  • ✨ Entry Fee: ¥400 (adults), ¥200 (children)
  • ✨ Contact: +81 3-3828-5644

Tokyo Disneyland

4.6/5 stars rating

Tokyo Disneyland is a magical theme park that brings classic fairy tales and beloved characters to life in a vibrant and joyful setting. Inspired by the original Disneyland Park, it features beautifully designed lands such as Fantasyland, Adventureland, and Tomorrowland. Visitors are captivated by iconic attractions like Pirates of the Caribbean and Space Mountain, as well as colorful parades and dazzling fireworks shows. The park's cheerful atmosphere, friendly staff, and attention to detail create an unforgettable experience for guests of all ages. Families especially enjoy meeting famous Disney characters and exploring the enchanting castle at the heart of the park. People visit Tokyo Disneyland to escape reality, relive childhood memories, and enjoy a day full of fun and imagination. Overall, it remains one of Tokyo's most popular destinations for both tourists and locals alike.

  • ✨ Entry Fee: ¥7,400 (adults), ¥4,800 (children)

TOKYO DISNEYSEA

4.5/5 stars rating

Tokyo DisneySea is one of the most unique and visually stunning theme parks in the world, inspired by the mysteries and beauty of the sea. Unlike typical amusement parks, it features seven themed ports such as Mediterranean Harbor and Mysterious Island, each designed with incredible detail and storytelling. Visitors are drawn to its breathtaking architecture, immersive atmosphere, and high-quality attractions that feel like stepping into a movie. Popular rides like Journey to the Center of the Earth and Soaring: Fantastic Flight offer thrilling and unforgettable experiences. The park is also known for its delicious themed food, creative shows, and seasonal events that make every visit special. Many people visit Tokyo DisneySea because it offers a more mature and magical experience, perfect for both families and adults. Overall, it stands out as a must-visit destination for anyone exploring Tokyo.

FUJI Q HIGHLAND

4.3/5 stars rating

Fuji-Q Highland is an exciting amusement park located near the base of Mount Fuji, offering breathtaking views along with world-class thrill rides. It is famous for its record-breaking roller coasters such as Fujiyama, Eejanaika, and Takabisha, which attract adrenaline seekers from around the world. The park combines extreme attractions with unique themed areas, including anime-inspired experiences and haunted houses. Visitors are drawn to the intense speed, steep drops, and innovative ride designs that create unforgettable moments. In addition to thrill rides, the scenic surroundings of Mount Fuji make the park visually stunning and perfect for photos. People visit Fuji-Q Highland for the ultimate adrenaline rush as well as the chance to enjoy entertainment in a one-of-a-kind location. Overall, it is a must-visit destination for adventure lovers exploring Japan.


  • ✨ Location: 5 Chome-6-Fuji-Q Highland, Fujiyoshida, Yamanashi Prefecture.
  • ✨ Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 10:00 PM (varies by season)
  • ✨ Entry Fee: ¥6,800 (adults), ¥3,400 (children)
  • ✨ Contact: +81 55-972-3333

PIZZA MARUMO

4.4/5 stars rating

Pizza Marumo is a popular and creative pizzeria located in Tokyo's Ebisu district, known for blending traditional Italian pizza with unique Japanese flavors. The restaurant is famous for its wood-fired Neapolitan-style pizzas, featuring both classic options like Margherita and innovative creations such as the Japanese Umami pizza. Visitors are drawn to its cozy atmosphere, where they can watch skilled chefs prepare pizzas right in front of them at the counter. The combination of fresh ingredients, soft and airy crust, and creative toppings makes every dish memorable. Many people visit Pizza Marumo to experience something different from ordinary pizza, where East meets West in a delicious way. Its growing reputation as one of Tokyo's top pizza spots also attracts food lovers from around the world. Overall, it offers a unique dining experience that combines quality, creativity, and comfort.
